Where It All Began

Sometimes the most powerful solutions come from the simplest observations during family visits.

During a vacation at my uncle's house, I noticed something that would change my perspective on an entire industry. My uncle owned several construction vehicles - excavators, bulldozers, and cranes - which served as his secondary source of income. But throughout my entire week-long stay, these expensive machines sat idle in his yard.

Curious, I asked him, "Why aren't these vehicles at work? Surely there must be construction sites that need them?"

His response was eye-opening: "No one called, and there's no construction happening locally that I know of."

This moment revealed a fundamental gap in the market. My uncle's only source of work was a phone call from someone in his network. But somewhere in the city, there were likely construction sites desperately looking for exactly the equipment he owned - they just didn't know how to reach him.
